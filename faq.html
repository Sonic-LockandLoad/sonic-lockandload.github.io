Frequently Asked Questions

Disclaimer: These questions were not actually frequently asked. Except the Big the Cat one.

  • Is Sonic: Lock & Load related at all to Sonic Robo Blast 2?
    • No - Sonic Robo Blast 2 is a completely different project from Sonic: Lock & Load.
    • SRB2 is a 3D platformer, not a first-person shooter. The only thing it remotely shares in common with S:L&L is that it's Doom-based.
    • S:L&L does take inspiration from SRB2, though.
  • Who even wanted a Sonic first-person shooter?
    • Me. I wanted it.
  • How do I play this thing?
    • You need GZDoom and a copy of DOOM. If you don't want to buy DOOM, Freedoom will work just as well.
    • You should use either DOOM II or Freedoom: Phase 2 if you want to play any Lock & Load specific content.
  • Why is Classic Sonic in the game?
    • I wanted to bring back the original style from Sonic the Hedgehog in DOOM, Sonic: Lock & Load's predecessor. He's not just a Sonic clone -- he's his own character.
  • Where can I ask about or complain about the mod?
    • You can leave an issue on the GitHub repo.
  • Why aren't Tails or Knuckles playable??
    • The former because I can't draw sprites for Tails that look good, and the latter because I don't know what gameplay style Knuckles would have.
  • Why do big rings only give you up to 300 rings?
    • Balancing reasons. Prior to S:L&L v1.0, they used to give you up to 999, which basically gave you enough health to practically just walk through every level nonchalantly.
    • In addition, the player character will take extra damage if their ring count exceeds 300. Again, for balancing.
  • Can you add Big the Cat?
    • No.
  • Why don't you make your own assets?
    • I'm not a talented artist. If I were able to, I would, but art, especially sprite art, is hard.
  • Why is Amy's in-game sprite based on her Classic design, but the art used is her Modern design?
    • Read the above.
  • Can I play Sonic: Lock & Load with a controller?
    • You can, but you probably shouldn't. S:L&L is really fast-paced and requires precision at high levels of play, not to mention additional inputs beyond just Fire and Alt-Fire which is better with keyboard and mouse. Of course, if you want to, you can play S:L&L with a controller on the highest difficulty, I won't stop you.
